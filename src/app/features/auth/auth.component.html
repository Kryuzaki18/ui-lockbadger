LockBadger uses bcrypt to secure your master password and applies an additional encryption layer on every stored credential — so even if the database is compromised, your data stays unreadable.

  • 🔒
    bcrypt master password

    Your master password is hashed with bcrypt — it is never stored in plain text and cannot be reversed.

  • 🛡️
    Double-encrypted credentials

    Every stored email, username and password gets an extra encryption pass on top of the vault — two locks, not one.

  • 🗄️
    Breach-resistant storage

    Raw credentials are never written to the database. A stolen dump reveals nothing without your key.

  • 🔑
    You hold the key

    Decryption happens on your end. No server ever sees your plaintext passwords.
